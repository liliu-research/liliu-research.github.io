My teaching interests are closely linked to materials science, micro-joining, electronic packaging, and reliability engineering. I have been involved in undergraduate and postgraduate teaching, curriculum development, student supervision, and teaching reform activities in materials forming and control engineering.

## Teaching Courses

- Principles and Methods of Micro-Joining
- Microelectronic Manufacturing Technology
- Materials and Structure Design of Modern Microelectronic Packaging
- Introduction to Materials
- Electronic packaging materials and reliability

## Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach emphasises the connection between fundamental materials science and real engineering applications. In courses related to micro-joining and electronic packaging, I aim to help students understand how interfacial reactions, microstructure evolution, processing conditions, and reliability requirements are connected in advanced manufacturing and power electronic systems.

I am particularly interested in project-based learning, case-based teaching, research-led teaching, and the integration of real engineering problems into materials and packaging education.

## Curriculum Development and Teaching Reform

My educational research focuses on project-based teaching, blended learning, and comparative curriculum development in materials and electronic packaging education.

Selected topics include:

- Blended teaching design for *Principles and Methods of Micro-Joining*
- Project-based learning in electronic packaging and micro-joining education
- Comparative studies of Chinese and international textbooks in electronic packaging
- Integration of research-led teaching into materials forming and packaging courses
- Quantitative evaluation of teaching effectiveness and student engagement
