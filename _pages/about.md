## Bio

Dr Li Liu is a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Microelectronics at Delft University of Technology, working with Prof. Willem D. van Driel and Prof. Guoqi Zhang.  Her research focuses on advanced electronic packaging, with particular interests in die-attachment materials and technologies, interfacial reliability, multi-physics reliability modelling, and advanced materials characterisation.

Before joining TU Delft, she was an Associate Professor and Principal Investigator in the School of Materials Science and Engineering at Wuhan University of Technology. She received her PhD in Electronics Manufacturing from Loughborough University, United Kingdom, and her BEng in Materials Forming and Control from Huazhong University of Science and Technology, China.

Her current MSCA project, **OrganVOID**, aims to develop antioxidant Cu sintering nanopastes for low-temperature, pressureless, and air-compatible die attachment in next-generation power electronics. She has led multiple research projects and published extensively in electronic packaging. Her current work at TU Delft builds on her previous independent research experience and aims to establish a European research line on reliable interconnects for next-generation advanced electronic packaging.
