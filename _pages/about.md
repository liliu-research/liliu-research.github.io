## Bio

Dr Li Liu is a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions Postdoctoral Fellow in the Electronic Components, Technology and Materials group at Delft University of Technology, working with Prof. Willem D. van Driel and Prof. Guoqi Zhang. Before joining TU Delft, she was an Associate Professor and Principal Investigator in the School of Materials Science and Engineering at Wuhan University of Technology.

Her research focuses on advanced electronic packaging, with particular interests in advanced electronic packaging, die-attachment materials and technologies, interfacial reliability, multi-physics reliability modelling, and advanced materials characterisation.

Her current MSCA project, **OrganVOID**, aims to develop antioxidant Cu sintering nanopastes for low-temperature, pressureless, and air-compatible die attachment in next-generation power electronic modules. The project integrates materials formulation, multi-scale simulations, and reliability testing.

She has led multiple research projects in electronic packaging and has published extensively in the areas of die-attach materials, interconnection technology, interfacial behaviour, and reliability modelling.
