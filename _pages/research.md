My research aims to develop reliable interconnect and die-attachment technologies for next-generation power electronic packaging. I am particularly interested in understanding how materials design, interfacial evolution, processing conditions, defect formation, and multi-physics loading jointly determine the reliability of advanced packaging structures.

## Research Vision

Power electronic modules are increasingly required to operate under higher temperature, higher power density, and more severe thermo-mechanical and electro-chemical environments. These requirements create critical challenges for die-attachment materials, interfacial stability, thermal management, and lifetime prediction.

My research vision is to establish a materials-to-reliability framework for advanced power electronic packaging, combining metallic sintering, interface science, advanced 3D characterisation, multi-scale simulation, and predictive reliability modelling.

## Research Themes

### 1. Low-temperature sintered interconnects

I develop Cu-, Ag-, and Cu/Ag-based sintered interconnects for high-temperature and high-reliability die attachment in power electronic modules. This research addresses key challenges including oxidation control, low-temperature bonding, pressureless sintering, porosity regulation, and interfacial bonding strength.

### 2. Interfacial reliability of power modules

I investigate interfacial reactions, void evolution, crack initiation, and degradation mechanisms in die-attach layers and metallised substrates under thermal, mechanical, and electrical loading. This work aims to clarify the relationships between interfacial microstructure, defect morphology, damage accumulation, and long-term reliability.

### 3. Multi-scale and multi-physics modelling

I combine finite element modelling, molecular dynamics, first-principles calculations, and data-driven approaches to understand and predict the reliability of electronic packaging materials and structures. My modelling work focuses on linking atomic-scale bonding, microstructural evolution, and device-level thermo-mechanical performance.

### 4. Advanced characterisation and defect-informed reliability

I use advanced characterisation techniques, including X-ray computed tomography, digital volume correlation, focused ion beam, and in-situ mechanical testing, to link microstructure, defects, strain evolution, and reliability performance. 
### 5. Advanced packaging reliability

Beyond conventional power module packaging, I am interested in reliability challenges in heterogeneous integration, chiplet packaging, and advanced interconnect architectures. My long-term goal is to develop robust interconnect and reliability solutions for next-generation electronic devices.
